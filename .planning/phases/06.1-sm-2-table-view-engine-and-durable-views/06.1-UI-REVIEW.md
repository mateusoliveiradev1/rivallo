# Phase 06.1 — UI Review

**Audited:** 2026-07-16
**Baseline:** approved `06.1-UI-SPEC.md`, `PRODUCT.md`, and `DESIGN.md`
**Evidence:** four tracked Playwright baselines inspected at original resolution; code and automated browser evidence audited
**Live server:** unavailable on ports 3000, 5173, and 8080; no new screenshots were captured
**Remediated:** 2026-07-16
**Release verdict:** **Phase 06.1 scope passed**, with focused visual polish and human approval deferred

## Executive Verdict

Phase 06.1 delivers a credible football-management workspace rather than another component lab. The native table, real faces, shirt numbers, contextual inspector, restrained dark teal-graphite surfaces, and dense row geometry align with Rivallo's “command room under match-night floodlights” direction.

The automated evidence is substantially better than the visual finish. Contrast, keyboard operation paths, focus visibility, reduced motion, 200% reflow, document overflow, and the ≥65% table allocation have meaningful browser coverage. Those passes must not be interpreted as visual approval.

Two major defects were recorded in the original audit:

1. The customizer is materially wider than its approved contract and, at 1024px, loses its visible heading/close affordance while covering the columns whose changes should be previewed.
2. Every visible column contributes three sequential focus targets and persistent move/resize chrome. With 17 visible columns, the header alone can expose 51 Tab stops, contradicting the bounded-tab-stop contract and making the data table look like a configuration tool instead of the primary football workspace.

The customizer defect is resolved within Phase 06.1. The bounded header focus/chrome finding and the remaining polish items stay explicitly deferred; no global shell redesign, route change, token replacement, or table rewrite is justified.

## Original Priority Fixes (Historical)

1. **Constrain and stabilize the customizer** — Use ~384px on desktop and ≤420px at 1024, preserve a fixed visible heading/close row, and keep the configured columns visible enough for immediate preview.
2. **Bound header keyboard traversal and quiet the chrome** — Introduce a roving header focus model and reveal move/resize affordances on hover/focus rather than drawing them permanently in every column.
3. **Restore one clear action hierarchy** — Keep `Salvar visualização` primary in the open customizer, demote `Adicionar filtro`, and ensure disabled/saved `Salvar escalação` does not read as another live primary action.

## Detailed Findings

### P1 — Major

#### UI-061-01 — Customizer geometry violates the approved responsive contract — RESOLVED

**Status:** Resolved in the Phase 06.1 stabilization. Browser coverage and the preserved 1024/1366/1920/2560 baselines confirm the responsive contract.

- **Pillars:** Visuals, Spacing, Experience Design
- **Locations:** `matchday.css:3180-3183`, `matchday.css:3711-3729`; all four tracked baselines
- **Contract:** UI-SPEC lines 170-172 require ≤420px at 1024 and around 384px on desktop.
- **Observed:** desktop width is `34rem` (544px); the compact sheet wrapper is `32rem` (512px).
- **Impact:** the overlay hides the exact table columns users are changing, weakening immediate preview. At 1024, the baseline also lacks a visible customizer heading and close control, leaving Escape as the least ambiguous exit.
- **Recommendation:** constrain the anchored surface to `24rem` and the compact sheet to no more than 420px. Make the popover root a two-row layout (`auto minmax(0, 1fr)`), keep the header/close row visible, and scroll only the content body. Add a browser assertion for visible heading and close action at 1024.
- **Suggested command:** `$impeccable layout apps/desktop/src/matchday`

#### UI-061-02 — Header focus order is unbounded and its control chrome overwhelms the data

- **Pillars:** Experience Design, Visuals, Accessibility
- **Locations:** `SquadWorkspace.tsx:814-865`, `matchday.css:3448-3454`
- **Contract:** UI-SPEC line 308 requires bounded Tab stops and internal navigation rather than traversal through every cell/control.
- **Observed:** each visible `<th>` contains a sort button, move button, and focusable resize separator. With 17 visible columns, the header can expose 51 Tab stops before row actions.
- **Impact:** keyboard users must perform excessive traversal; pointer users see a dense row of identical outlined `↔` controls that becomes louder than player identities and statistics.
- **Recommendation:** use one roving Tab stop for the active header/column group. Arrow Left/Right should move between columns; Enter or a compact action menu can enter sort/move/resize modes. Preserve all current keyboard commands and announcements. Keep handles visually quiet until `:hover`/`:focus-within`, without making them pointer-only.
- **WCAG/standard:** not necessarily a standalone WCAG failure, but it materially conflicts with efficient logical focus order and the approved interaction contract.
- **Suggested command:** `$impeccable distill apps/desktop/src/matchday/SquadWorkspace.tsx`

### P2 — Important

#### UI-061-03 — Ultrawide inspector grows instead of preserving the table-first hierarchy

- **Pillars:** Spacing, Visuals
- **Locations:** `matchday.css:3840-3852`; 2560×1080 baseline
- **Contract:** UI-SPEC line 95 fixes the open inspector at 304px unless reflow is active.
- **Observed:** at ≥2200px, the inspector becomes 340px.
- **Impact:** additional screen width is partially assigned to a secondary panel rather than the primary table, contrary to the ultrawide hierarchy rule.
- **Recommendation:** retain 304px at ultrawide sizes and let the table absorb the difference. This is a one-rule correction, not a layout redesign.
- **Suggested command:** `$impeccable layout apps/desktop/src/matchday/matchday.css`

#### UI-061-04 — Too many green action surfaces compete simultaneously

- **Pillars:** Color, Visuals
- **Locations:** UI-SPEC lines 124 and 131; `MatchdayScreen.tsx:1309-1311`; `SquadWorkspace.tsx:649-668` and `1102-1110`; `TableViewCustomizer.tsx:664-670`
- **Observed:** the open state can show green `Continuar`, `Adicionar filtro`, `Salvar visualização`, XI badges, progress bars, and a green-toned disabled `Salvar escalação`.
- **Impact:** users do not get one immediate “next action”; semantic success, selection, and primary commitment converge on the same visual weight.
- **Recommendation:** make `Adicionar filtro` secondary/info, keep `Salvar visualização` as the customizer's only primary commit action, and neutralize the disabled/saved lineup action more strongly. Preserve green XI/status semantics through shape and text.
- **Suggested command:** `$impeccable colorize apps/desktop/src/matchday`

#### UI-061-05 — Customizer title and heading hierarchy drift from the contract

- **Pillars:** Copywriting, Typography
- **Locations:** UI-SPEC lines 109 and 263; `TableViewCustomizer.tsx:467`; `primitives.css:491-496`
- **Observed:** the surface heading is `Configurar tabela`, while the contract calls for `Personalizar tabela`; the shared popover heading renders at 14px rather than the 16px section-heading role.
- **Impact:** trigger and surface repeat the same phrase, reducing orientation and making the overlay feel like a large menu rather than a focused editing workspace.
- **Recommendation:** keep trigger copy `Configurar tabela`, change the surface title to `Personalizar tabela`, and give that title the 16px/600 composite heading role without changing global popover typography.
- **Suggested command:** `$impeccable typeset apps/desktop/src/matchday/TableViewCustomizer.tsx`

#### UI-061-06 — Width editing is accessible but visually cryptic

- **Pillars:** Experience Design, Copywriting, Visuals
- **Locations:** `TableViewCustomizer.tsx:576-586`, `matchday.css:3339-3358`
- **Observed:** users see a long dark track, a thin center line, and `56–80px`, but no persistent `Largura` label or visible current-value control beside the track.
- **Impact:** keyboard users receive a strong accessible name, while pointer users may not recognize the region as draggable or understand the 8/24px step model.
- **Recommendation:** add visible `Largura · 64px`, strengthen the handle without adding decorative chrome, and expose a concise focus hint or ±8 controls. Keep the existing separator role and keyboard behavior.
- **Suggested command:** `$impeccable clarify apps/desktop/src/matchday/TableViewCustomizer.tsx`

#### UI-061-07 — Versioned visual evidence covers only one state

- **Pillars:** Visuals, Experience Design
- **Locations:** the four `table-view-*.png` baselines; `browser-tests/matchday.spec.ts:1458-1464`
- **Observed:** all four PNGs capture a dirty user view with the customizer open and the search narrowed to `Idade`.
- **Impact:** responsive consistency is proven, but human visual quality is not demonstrated for the normal closed workspace, focus-visible operation, empty result, save failure, repository unavailable, or recovery states.
- **Recommendation:** add a deliberately small review set rather than snapshotting every permutation: closed default at 1366, keyboard focus/move at 1366, save-failure or future-recovery state, and 1024 empty/reflow state. Keep the current computed contrast matrix.
- **Suggested command:** `$impeccable audit apps/desktop/src/matchday`

## Pillar Detail

### 1. Copywriting — 3/4

What works:

- lifecycle verbs identify their object (`Salvar visualização`, `Restaurar colunas`, `Descartar ajustes`);
- no generic `OK`, `Cancelar`, or objectless `Salvar` was introduced in the audited composites;
- empty, failure, migration, and recovery states explain impact and next action in Portuguese;
- provenance and dirty state use visible text, not color alone.

Why it is not 4:

- the customizer heading differs from the approved copy;
- width adjustment lacks a visible task label;
- the provisional English shell subtitle interrupts language consistency.

### 2. Visuals — 2/4

What works:

- the table is unmistakably the football-management core;
- player faces, position badges, flags, XI status, and inspector facts create a truthful scan path;
- no global redesign, nested-card proliferation, decorative glass, or copied manager skin appeared.

Why it is 2:

- the open customizer obscures rather than supports live preview;
- permanent move/resize controls dominate the header;
- the 1024 sheet loses visible orientation/close affordance;
- current baselines do not show the normal closed workspace.

### 3. Color — 3/4

What works:

- the dark teal-graphite ground is coherent with Rivallo;
- computed WCAG AA thresholds pass for text, controls, and focus;
- amber dirty/attention, cyan information, red danger, and green action/success remain semantically recognizable;
- the Phase 06.1 CSS section uses semantic tokens rather than adding raw color values.

Why it is not 4:

- too many simultaneous green actions and positive indicators flatten focal hierarchy, particularly while the customizer is open.

### 4. Typography — 3/4

What works:

- Inter is used operationally;
- table/meta text remains at least 12px;
- row data and screen headings remain fixed across ultrawide viewports;
- data uses compact 12/14px rhythm and tabular numerals rather than display typography.

Why it is not 4:

- the customizer's primary heading inherits the generic 14px popover title rather than the contracted 16px composite heading, weakening the surface hierarchy.

### 5. Spacing — 2/4

What works:

- 4/8/12/16/24px rhythm is consistent in controls and rows;
- 44/48/54px density is preserved;
- table-local overflow and responsive inspector reflow prevent document amplification.

Why it is 2:

- 544px desktop and 512px compact customizer widths miss explicit targets by large margins;
- the 340px ultrawide inspector contradicts the 304px fixed-secondary-panel rule;
- a one-result search leaves excessive unused space between the column control and footer actions.

### 6. Experience Design — 2/4

What works:

- saved-view lifecycle, dirty transitions, immutable provenance, save retry, corruption/future recovery, and legacy migration are unusually complete;
- focus return, operation rollback, live announcements, semantic table relationships, and reduced-motion behavior are implemented;
- the table remains usable when repository features fail.

Why it is 2:

- the customizer blocks immediate visual verification of edits;
- keyboard access exists but creates an excessive sequential traversal surface;
- pointer resize discoverability is weak;
- important product states have semantic tests but insufficient manual visual evidence.
